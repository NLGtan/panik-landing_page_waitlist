USDC Depeg — SVB Crisis

March 10–13, 2023 · Aave V3 Ethereum · 102 wallets affected

Silicon Valley Bank (SVB) collapsed on March 10, 2023. Circle, the company behind USDC, had $3.3 billion in deposits at SVB. When news broke, USDC lost its $1 peg — dropping as low as $0.87 — proving that even "safe" stablecoins can fail. People using USDC as loan collateral were suddenly at risk of liquidation.

The stablecoin that wasn't stable: USDC price, Mar 9–14, 2023

USDC is supposed to be worth exactly $1 at all times — that's the entire point of a stablecoin. When SVB collapsed, USDC dropped 13 cents in a single day. For anyone using it as loan collateral, that triggered automatic liquidations they couldn't have predicted.

When did PANIK warn vs when did the crisis hit?

PANIK detected danger 2 days before the worst liquidations. Here's why: positions close to their liquidation threshold were already flagged CRITICAL on Mar 9 — before SVB news broke. The health-factor floor caught them automatically because their safety buffer was already thin.

Mar 9
PANIK flags CRITICAL — Positions at HF ≤ 1.10 automatically receive warnings. USDC is still at $1.00.
Mar 10
SVB collapses — News breaks. USDC begins to depeg. Price drops from $1.00 to ~$0.94.
Mar 11
USDC hits $0.87 — Aave oracle registers the drop. Positions liquidated. Most PANIK-warned users already had 48 hours to act.
Mar 13
US gov backstops SVB — USDC recovers back toward $1.00.

Bottom line

This event demonstrated something counterintuitive: PANIK doesn't need to predict bank failures to protect you from them. Because PANIK continuously monitors how close your position is to liquidation — in any market — positions with thin safety buffers were already flagged CRITICAL on March 9, a full 2 days before the worst liquidations. PANIK caught 97 out of 100 at-risk positions. The 30% false-alarm rate reflects that many positions were genuinely close to the edge during the depeg, even if they ultimately survived as USDC recovered.
