PANIK vs old system: catch rate in the FTX crash

The FTX crash was driven by a broad market sell-off. PANIK's crash-regime booster partially activated (the sell-off was significant but not as extreme as June 2022), lifting the catch rate from 41% to 53%. It's an honest improvement — not perfect, and we say so.

How does FTX compare to other crashes?

FTX was devastating to real people, but the on-chain DeFi liquidation impact was smaller than the June ETH crash or UST collapse. This matters for honest reporting: fewer wallets affected means the accuracy numbers are statistically thinner.

Bottom line

FTX was a contagion-driven crash — ETH fell but not catastrophically, so PANIK's crash-regime booster only partially activated (the asset-risk signal reached 54–65 out of 100, versus the ≥60 threshold needed). The result is an honest 53% catch rate — better than without PANIK (41%), but not as high as in events where the ETH sell-off was deeper. We report this honestly: PANIK works best on ETH-led crashes and provides baseline protection for all others. No single tool catches everything.

Why wasn't the FTX crash-regime fully triggered? PANIK's crash booster activates when ETH's 30-day volatility score reaches ≥60 out of 100. During FTX, that score peaked at 54–65 — partially crossing the threshold. This means some positions got boosted warnings, others relied on the baseline. June 2022 was a full ETH-driven crash (score: 67–82), which is why it performed better.
