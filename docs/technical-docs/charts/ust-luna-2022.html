UST / LUNA Collapse

May 9–13, 2022 · Aave V2 Ethereum · 1,353 wallets affected

UST was supposed to be a "safe" $1 stablecoin backed algorithmically by LUNA. In four days it went from $1 to near-zero — one of the fastest and most destructive collapses in crypto history. People using UST as collateral for loans lost everything almost instantly.

The collapse: UST price, May 8–14, 2022

UST was designed to always be worth $1. When the peg broke on May 9th, the selling pressure created a death spiral. By May 13th, UST was worth less than a penny. Anyone with UST-backed loans had no time to react.

Did PANIK catch the positions that blew up?

Even in a crash this sudden, PANIK's static health-factor floor caught 94 out of 100 at-risk positions. The crash was too fast for the "crash-regime" booster to activate, but the baseline protection worked. Only 1 in 5 safe positions received an unnecessary warning.

Bottom line

UST was a "lightning" crash — so fast that even our crash-regime booster (which needs 30 days of price data to confirm a sell-off) never activated. Yet PANIK still caught 94 out of 100 positions heading for liquidation, because the basic health-factor floor triggered as positions got dangerously close to the edge. The 20% false-alarm rate — the lowest of any event tested — shows that in a crisis this fast, PANIK's conservative approach doesn't over-react. The key lesson: PANIK's core protection works independently of market regime detection, making it robust against sudden collapses.
