Yen Carry Trade Unwind

August 5, 2024 · Base Chain · Aave V3 + Moonwell + Morpho Blue + Compound III

On August 5, 2024, the Bank of Japan unexpectedly raised interest rates. Traders who had borrowed cheaply in yen to buy crypto were forced to unwind their positions all at once — triggering a global flash crash. ETH dropped ~23% in a single day. This was the first major crash that hit PANIK's home network, Base, and it tested all four DeFi protocols PANIK monitors.

How many hours of warning did each protocol's users get?

PANIK monitors four different DeFi lending protocols on Base. Each one had slightly different loan structures, but all received meaningful advance warning — ranging from 12 to 33 hours before liquidations happened. That's the window to act: add collateral, repay some debt, or exit.

Did PANIK catch every at-risk position across all four protocols?

Aave V3 was validated with a full survivor analysis (comparing flagged vs unflagged positions for both liquidated AND surviving borrowers). The other three were validated for recall — confirming every liquidated position received a CRITICAL warning before it happened.

Bottom line

This was the proving ground for PANIK on its home network. Across 6,051 tested wallets and four different DeFi protocols, PANIK's engine generalized cleanly — no protocol-specific tuning was needed. The crash was an intraday flash (ETH crashed and partly recovered the same day), so the crash-regime booster didn't activate (ETH's 30-day volatility score peaked at 52, just below the ≥60 threshold). The static health-factor floor alone delivered 12–33 hours of warning across all protocols. That's PANIK's baseline protection working exactly as intended.

What's the difference between 92% and 100%? The 92% on Aave V3 comes from a stricter test — we checked both liquidated AND surviving positions, measuring how often PANIK warned unnecessarily (false-alarms). The 100% on the other three protocols measures recall only (did every liquidated position get flagged?). A position that reaches HF=1.0 will always trip the floor — so 100% recall for liquidated positions is expected. The more honest number that includes survivors (false-alarm rate) is only available for Aave V3 so far.
